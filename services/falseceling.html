Parallel Workflow

Ceiling & Painting — Side by Side

We run ceiling and painting work in parallel tracks to deliver your project in minimum time.

False Ceiling Track
Day 1
Layout Marking

Mark ceiling level, light points, AC vents on walls

Day 2
Framework Installation

Fix GI channels at marked level, install cross tees

Day 3–4
Board Fixing

Cut and screw gypsum boards to framework

Day 5
Joint Finishing

Tape joints, apply 2 coats of compound, sand smooth

Day 6
Primer Coat

Apply ceiling primer, cut openings for lights/vents

Painting Track
Day 1
Surface Preparation

Wall cleaning, putty patching, crack filling

Day 2–3
Putty Work (2 Coats)

Apply 2 coats of wall putty, sand between coats

Day 4
Primer Application

1 coat of primer for adhesion and uniformity

Day 5–6
1st & 2nd Coat Paint

Apply 2 coats of emulsion with drying time between

Day 7
Touch-Up & Cleanup

Final touch-ups, edge cutting, masking removal

Avoid These

Common Ceiling & Painting Mistakes

These are the mistakes we see in DIY or low-quality work. We ensure none of these happen on your project.

Our Quality Guarantee

Every project goes through a 7-point quality checklist before handover — including joint inspection, paint coverage test, light alignment check, and edge finish verification.

1
Visible Joints

Poor taping and insufficient compound coats leave visible seam lines on the ceiling.

2
Skipping Putty

Painting directly on plaster without putty leads to uneven absorption and patchy appearance.

3
Wrong Primer

Using interior primer on exterior or skipping primer entirely causes premature paint failure.

4
Uneven Level

Ceiling marked at wrong height or framework not leveled — leads to a visibly tilted ceiling.

5
No Light Planning

Installing ceiling without pre-planning light positions leads to ugly retrofits and exposed wires.

6
Single Paint Coat

Applying only one coat to save cost results in poor coverage, streaks, and uneven color.

FAQ

Ceiling & Painting Questions

Common queries about false ceiling materials, painting processes, and maintenance.

Ask Your Question
How long does a false ceiling last?
A well-installed gypsum false ceiling lasts 15-20 years without any issues. The key factors are quality of framework (GI thickness), proper screw spacing, and joint finishing. We use 0.40mm GI framework which ensures long-term stability. Popcorn or textured ceilings may need refreshing every 8-10 years.
Does false ceiling reduce room height?
Yes, typically 4-6 inches of height is lost. For a standard 10ft ceiling, the finished height becomes approximately 9.5ft. For low-height rooms (below 9ft), we recommend perimeter tray ceilings that drop only the edges, keeping the center at full height while still hiding services.
How many coats of paint are actually needed?
The standard professional process is: 2 coats putty + 1 coat primer + 2 coats emulsion = 5 total layers. Some dark colors may need 3 coats of emulsion. We never skip steps — each layer serves a specific purpose (filling, sealing, color coverage, durability).
Can AC vents and fire sprinklers be integrated?
Absolutely — this is one of the primary reasons for installing a false ceiling. We coordinate with your AC contractor and fire safety team to create precise cutouts for supply/return vents, cassette AC units, fire sprinklers, smoke detectors, and CCTV cameras. All openings are finished with proper trim rings.
What's the difference between POP and gypsum ceiling?
POP (Plaster of Paris) is hand-applied wet material — great for decorative elements like medallions, cornices, and curved designs but slower and messier. Gypsum boards are factory-made panels screwed to a metal frame — faster, cleaner, more consistent, and better for large flat areas. We use both depending on the design requirement.
Is false ceiling safe during earthquakes?
Gypsum false ceilings are lightweight (approximately 8-10 kg per sq meter vs 240 kg for brick) and flex with the building during seismic activity. They don't add significant load to the structure. However, they can crack at joints during strong tremors — which is repairable. We use proper cross-bracing in the framework for added stability.
