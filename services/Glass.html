Installation

Day-by-Day Timeline

A typical 3,000 sq ft office partition project — from marking to handover in just 10 working days.

Parallel Work Streams

We run ceiling, flooring, and electrical teams in parallel with partition work to compress overall project timelines significantly.

Site Marking & Layout Day 1

Mark partition lines on floor and ceiling as per approved drawing. Verify electrical points, door positions, and AC duct locations.

Frame & Track Installation Day 2–3

Install aluminium U-channels at floor and ceiling for glass. For gypsum — erect GI stud framing at marked positions.

Glass Cutting & Delivery Day 3–4

Toughened glass panels cut to size at factory, delivered to site. Gypsum boards measured and cut on-site for precise fitting.

Panel Fixing Day 5–7

Glass panels lifted and fixed into channels with silicone. Gypsum boards screwed to steel frame with joint compound applied.

Door & Hardware Fitting Day 8

Glass doors with patch fittings, floor springs, and locks installed. Gypsum doors framed and hung with hardware.

Finishing & Cleanup Day 9–10

Silicone cleanup, joint finishing, touch-up painting for gypsum, blind installation for glass, and full site cleanup for handover.

FAQ

Partition-Specific Questions

Common queries about glass and gypsum partition materials, installation, and maintenance.

Ask Your Question
Is toughened glass safe for office partitions?
Absolutely. We use 12mm toughened (tempered) glass that is 4-5 times stronger than regular glass. If it breaks, it shatters into small, blunt granules instead of sharp shards — making it completely safe for office environments. All our glass is IS 2553 certified.
Can I add blinds or frosted film to glass partitions?
Yes, both options are available. We offer integrated between-glass blinds (sealed within double-glazed units), manual roller blinds, and frosted/etched vinyl films. Films can be full frosted, gradient, or custom-patterned with your branding.
How much weight can a glass partition hold?
A standard 12mm toughened glass partition with proper U-channel fixing can safely hold light loads like blinds, small shelves, and whiteboards. For heavy loads (TV screens, storage), we use reinforced framed systems with additional support brackets.
Can gypsum partitions be removed later?
Standard gypsum partitions are fixed structures and removal involves demolition — but they cause minimal damage compared to brick walls since they're lightweight. If you need re-locatable walls, we recommend our demountable partition system which can be unbolted and re-installed elsewhere.
Do partitions need ceiling to be done first?
Not necessarily. In most projects, we install partitions and false ceiling in parallel — the partition top channel integrates with the ceiling grid. This parallel approach saves 5-7 days compared to sequential work. Our team coordinates both work streams simultaneously.
What's the difference between acoustic and regular gypsum?
Regular gypsum partitions use single 12.5mm boards on both sides of a steel frame — providing ~30 dB sound reduction. Acoustic partitions use double boards (2×12.5mm on each side) with mineral wool insulation inside the cavity — achieving 40-50 dB reduction, making conversations inaudible outside.
